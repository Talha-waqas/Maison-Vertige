Our Story

The Maison

Founded 2012

A Vision of Elevation

Maison Vertige was established by architect-turned-designer Isabelle Vertige. During her architectural practice in Paris, she constantly observed the structural physics of lines, cantilevers, and force distributions. This obsession naturally translated to high heels, which she viewed as the ultimate form of wearable architecture.

To master the heritage craft, Isabelle relocated to Florence, spending years studying shoemaking alongside master cobblers. In 2012, she opened a small studio in Milan to introduce engineered luxury stilettos to the fashion world.

Philosophy

Where Architecture Meets Movement

We reject the idea that beauty must dictate discomfort. Every stiletto we design is balanced using precision engineering, calculations, and pressure tests to maximize weight distribution across the arch.

From the curvature of our signature clear acrylic heel to the density of our dual-density cushioned insoles, every single component is designed to elevate both a woman's stature and her sense of comfort.

127 Steps

Craftsmanship

01

The Last

Every single model begins with a custom-carved wooden last, designed to create our signature architectural toe shape and pitch. This form determines the fit, balance, and visual lines of the finished stiletto.

02

The Cut

Our skins are individually inspected and cut by hand by master leather artisans in Milan. Only the most flawless segments are chosen, minimizing waste while highlighting natural grain patterns.

03

The Assembly

Over three days of meticulous production, our heels go through 127 steps of assembly. This includes setting our signature sculpted acrylic heel, hand-stretching the uppers, and positioning cushioning foam.

"I don't design shoes. I design the way a woman walks into a room."
— Isabelle Vertige, Founder
